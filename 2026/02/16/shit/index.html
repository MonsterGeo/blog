Prove for number n is Fibonacci number if and only if 5n2 ± 4 is perfect square number

Body

Fibonacci sequence

defintion 1(Fibonacci sequence)

For n > 1. $The sequence {Fn} satisfying Fn = Fn − 1 + Fn − 2 is called the Fibonacci sequence.where F0 = 0, F1 = 1.

Proposition 1(Binet’s formula)

For n > 0. The Fibonacci number Fn satisfying

$$ F_n = \frac{1}{5} (\gamma^n - \delta^n) $$

where $\gamma = \frac{1}{2}(1+\sqrt{5})$, $\delta = \frac{1}{2}(1-\sqrt{5})$

Proof: We by induction on n to prove it.

For base case $F_0 = \frac{1}{5}(\gamma^0 - \delta^0) = 0$.

By inductive step, assum the formula holds for all integers up to n,n-1.

$$ F_n = \frac{1}{5} (\gamma^n - \delta^n) , F_{n-1} = \frac{1}{5} (\gamma^{n-1} - \delta^{n-1}) $$

We have

$$ \begin{aligned} F_{n+1} =& F_n + F_{n-1}\\ =& \frac{1}{5} (\gamma^{n-1}(\gamma+1) - \delta^{n-1}(\delta+1))\\ =& \frac{1}{5} (\gamma^{n-1}\cdot \gamma^2 - \delta^{n-1}\cdot \delta^2) && \text{since } 1+\gamma = \gamma^2,1+\delta = \delta^2\\ =& \frac{1}{5} (\gamma^{n+1} - \delta^{n+1}) \end{aligned} $$

Cassini’s Formual

Let n > 1. Then Fn − 1Fn + 1 − Fn2 = (−1)n

Proof: By base step.F0F2 − F12 = 0 ⋅ 1 − 1 = (−1)1.

Now assume it is true for an arbitrary positive integer k. Then

$$ \begin{aligned} F_kF_{k+2}-F_{k+1}^2 =& (F_{k+1}-F_{k-1})(F_k+F_{k+1}) - F^2_{k+1}\\ =& F_kF_{k+1}-F_kF_{k-1}-[F_k^2+(-1)^k]\\ =& F_kF_{k+1}-F_k(F_{k-1}+F_k)+(-1)^{k+1}\\ =& F_kF_{k+1}-F_kF_{k+1}+(-1)^{k+1}\\ =& (-1)^{k+1} \end{aligned} $$

Q.E.D.

defintion 2: Lucas number

For$ n> 0$, the number Ln satisfying

$$ L_n = \begin{cases} 2 & n = 0\\ 1 & n = 1\\ L(n-2)+L_(n-1) & n> 1 \end{cases} $$

Call it Lucas number.

Proposition 2

For n > 0, we have Ln = γn + δn

For $ n = 0, n = 1$, we have

$$ L_0 = 2,L_1 = \frac{1+\sqrt{5}+1-\sqrt{5}}{2} = 1 $$

By inductive step. Assume it is true for an arbitrary positive integer n-1. Then

$$ \begin{array}{lll} L_n = L_{n-2} + L_{n-1} &=& \gamma^{n-2}+\delta^{n-2}+\gamma^{n-1} + \delta^{n-1}\\ &=& \gamma^{n-2}(\gamma+1) + \delta^{n-2}(\delta+1)\\ &=& \gamma^{n}+ \delta^n \end{array} $$

Q.E.D.

Lemma 1(Fn + 1 + Fn − 1 = Ln)

Proof: Use proposition 2 and Cassini’s formula.we have

Base step F0 + F2 = 0 + 1 = L1

by inductive step:

$$ \begin{array}{lll} L_{n+1} &=& L_n+L_{n-1}\\ &=& F_{n+1} + F_{n-1} + F_n+F_{n-2}\\ &=& (F_{n+1}+F_{n}) + (F_{n-1} + F_{n-2})\\ &=& F_{n+2} + F_{n}\\ \end{array} $$

Q.E.D.

Pell’s equation

For positive integer d that is not a square, an equation of the form x2 − dy2 = 1 is called Pell’s equation.

By Theorem 4.1 and Corollary 4.2 (see Theorem 4.1 and Corollary 4.2 in Pell’s equation 1) for any solution (x, y), (x′, y′) $(x+y\sqrt{d})(x'+y'\sqrt{d})$ and any form $(x+y\sqrt{d})^k$ are also a solution for all k ∈ ℤ.

Last, by Theorem 5.3(see Theorem 5.3 in Pell’s equation 1), all soulation can be generated form (x1, y1) where y1 are minimal by taking integral powers of $(x_1+y_1\sqrt{d})$ $$ x+y\sqrt{d} = \pm(x_1+y_1\sqrt{d})^k $$

main proposition

Proposition 4 n is Fibonacci number if and only if 5n2 ± 4 is perfect square number

Proof: Use lemma 1 and Cassini’s Formual

we have

$$ \begin{array}{rll} L_n &=& F_{n+1} +F_{n-1}\\ F^2_n+(-1)^n &=& F_{n+1}F_{n-1}\\ L^2_n - 4[F_n^2+(-1)^n] &=& (F_{n+1}+F_{n-1})^2 - 4F_{n+1}F_{n-1}\\ &=& (F_{n+1}-F_{n-1})^2\\ &=& F_n^2\\ L_n^2 &=& 5F_n^2+4(-1)^n \end{array} $$

So, $5n^2 $ equal square number.

Conversely,Suppose 5n2 ± 4 is square number m,then

m2 − 5n2 = ±4

We actually is find the solution (m, n) satisfying $\frac{m^2-5n^2}{4} = \pm 1$ , where n is minimal. A fundamental solution is (3, 1) and (1, 1) where (3, 1) is a solution of $\frac{m^2-5n^2}{4} =1$. (1, 1) is solution of $\frac{m^2-5n^2}{4} = -1$. For any integer k, we have $$ m_{k_1} = \frac{(3+\sqrt{5})^k + (3-\sqrt{5})^k}{2} $$

and

$$ n_{k_1} = \frac{(3+\sqrt{5})^k - (3-\sqrt{5})^k}{2\sqrt{5}} $$

and for another equation we have

$$ m_{k_2} = \frac{(1+\sqrt{5})^n + (1-\sqrt{5})^n}{2} $$

and

$$ n_{k_2} = \frac{(1+\sqrt{5})^n - (1-\sqrt{5})^n}{2\sqrt{5}} $$

Finally, note the $((1+\sqrt{5})/2)^2 = (3+\sqrt{5})/2$ and $((1- \sqrt{5} )/2)^2 = (3-\sqrt{5})/2$

Then $m_{k_1} = \frac{(2\cdot (3+\sqrt{5}) \cdot (1/2))^k + (2\cdot(3-\sqrt{5})\cdot 1/2)^k}{2} = \frac{(2\gamma^2)^k + (2\delta^2)^k}{2} = 2^{k-1}L_{2k}$, nk1 = 2k − 1F2k

For mk2 = 2k − 1Lk and nk2 = 2k − 1Fk

Therefore, for all k, the collection of all Fk and F2k forms the Fibonacci sequence
